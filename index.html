About Me

I am an applied mathematician working in mathematical biology. I use mathematical, computational, and statistical tools to determine how biological systems function and how they are organized.

In Fall 2021, I will be a PIMS Postdoctoral Fellow at The University of British Columbia (UBC) working with Khanh Dao Duc on understanding the structure heterogeneities of the ribosome through mRNA transport and Cryo-EM data.

Currently, I am an NSERC Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Vanderbilt University working in collaboration with William Holmes. Our work focuses on understanding how interactions between signaling molecules (Rho GTPases and PAR proteins) and the cytoskeleton control cell behavior. I was also the Senior Co-Chair of the Vanderbilt Postdoctoral Association for 2019–2020.

I completed a Ph.D. in Mathematics in 2018 at UBC, advised by Leah Edelstein-Keshet. My work focused on a broad set of biological systems: cell signaling and mechanics, intracellular transport by molecular motors, and collective cell migration in development.

I enjoy teaching mathematics and working collaboratively in teams. When I am not thinking about math or teaching, I can be found cycling, alpine or cross-country skiing, or drinking coffee.

Teaching

I enjoy teaching and working with others on developing instructional skills. I received a Killam Graduate Teaching Assistant Award in the 2016/2017 academic year, and have worked as a facilitator of Instructional Skills Workshops and as peer reviewer of teaching at the Centre for Teaching, Learning and Technology at UBC. I co-organized a community of practice, Graduate Student Instructor Support Group, with Matt Coles.

I volunteered with the Future Science Leaders program at Science World in Vancouver, BC, and worked as a Tutor for Indigenous Students in Science Courses at the UBC First Nations Longhouse. As an undergraduate student, I developed a series of web-based instructional modules (no longer available online) for the teaching and learning of modern applications of mathematics with Gerda de Vries.
